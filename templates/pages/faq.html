Frequently asked questions

Everything you need to know about InfinityCard.

What is InfinityCard?

InfinityCard is an NFC-powered digital identity platform. You receive a physical NFC card that, when tapped on any smartphone, instantly opens your personalized profile page — no app required.

How does the NFC card work?

Your NFC card contains a unique chip linked to your profile. When someone taps their phone on it, the phone's browser opens your profile URL automatically. Works with most modern smartphones.

Do people need an app to view my profile?

No. Your profile is a regular web page. It opens directly in the phone's browser — no downloads, no QR scanning, no friction.

What profile categories are available?

We offer 7 industry-specific templates: Business/Entrepreneur, Freelancer/Consultant, Real Estate Agent, Restaurant/Food Business, Creative (Photo/Video), Events (Catering/Decor), and General Personal. Each has custom fields and layouts for your industry.

Can I change my profile after creating it?

Absolutely. Your profile is fully editable from your dashboard at any time. Update your info, photos, theme, social links — your NFC card always points to the latest version.

What subscription plans do you offer?

We offer three plans: Starter ($3/mo), Business ($5/mo), and Pro ($10/mo). Every new account starts with a free 30-day trial. View plans.

What happens when my subscription expires?

Your profile page becomes temporarily unavailable to visitors. Your data is preserved — simply renew your subscription from the dashboard to reactivate everything.

Can I customize the look of my profile?

Yes. You can choose from several preset themes, each with its own color scheme. Pro plan users get access to all themes including dark mode options.

How do I get an NFC card?

After signing up and setting up your profile, you can order an NFC card from your dashboard. Cards are shipped with your unique profile link pre-programmed.

Is my data secure?

Yes. We use HTTPS encryption, CSRF protection, secure password hashing, and follow Django security best practices. Your data is stored in a secure PostgreSQL database.
